## Executive Summary

To ensure that **Sadhana** is built not as a generic wellness app, but as a premium, high-retention sanctuary, we have modeled three primary target personas. These personas are not demographic composites; they are psychographic representations of real behavioral needs, identity commitments, and functional/emotional/social jobs.

*   **Sarah (Busy Achiever):** Driven by competence and autonomy. Needs brief, high-performance routines. iPhone user.
*   **Michael (Authenticity Seeker):** Driven by self-actualization and status/belonging. Needs philosophical depth, lineage connection, and Sanskrit terminology. Android user.
*   **Elena (Restorative Senior):** Driven by security and autonomy. Needs physical restoration, joint mobility, clear accessibility, and low-cognitive-load audio. Android user.

## Persona 1: Sarah — The "Busy Achiever"

```
Name: Sarah Jenkins
Age: 32
Occupation: Tech Startup Founder & Chief Executive
Location: San Francisco, California (US)
Tech Comfort: Expert (Uses complex SaaS, wearables, smart home devices daily)
Device: iPhone 15 Pro (iOS 17+, 6.1-inch screen)
Subscription Tier: Free (highly likely to convert to Premium for convenience)
```

### 1. Visual Photo Description (for Image Generation)
*A high-resolution, candid close-up of a 32-year-old Caucasian female startup executive sitting in a modern, naturally lit office. She has a focused yet slightly fatigued expression, looking down at her phone. She has brown hair pulled back in a neat bun, wearing a sleek charcoal mock-neck sweater. The background is softly blurred with minimalist wooden shelves and a hint of a green potted plant (Earth Premium depth of field).*

### 2. Psychographic Profile (Identity-Need Map)
*   **Dominant Need State:** **Autonomy** & **Competence**. Sarah wants to feel in absolute control of her time and physical state, maintaining peak mental performance under extreme work stress.
*   **Identity Commitments:** She views herself as an ultra-productive, resilient, and modern "high-performer" who optimizes every aspect of life. She refuses to be seen as lazy, undisciplined, or traditional/outdated. She values evidence-based, scientifically backed wellness.
*   **Fears & Friction:**
    *   *Rational Fears:* Wasting time on long, slow routines; losing productivity; injury due to unguided postures; complex navigation.
    *   *Emotional Threats:* Burnout, imposter syndrome, losing her "edge" due to brain fog or lack of sleep.
*   **Worldview & Values:** Life is a series of optimization opportunities. Modern problems (stress, sleep loss) require modern, efficient solutions. Authentic traditions are valuable, but only if they are formatted to respect her busy schedule.

### 3. Jobs-to-be-Done (Progress Job Decomposition)
*   **Hiring Trigger:** Waking up at 5:30 AM with a racing mind, neck tension from desk work, and a calendar packed with back-to-back meetings, feeling that the day is controlling her rather than she controlling the day.
*   **Progress State:**
    *   *Before:* High-cortisol, reactive "fight-or-flight" state, physical stiffness, and anxiety about the day's tasks.
    *   *After:* Grounded, focused, physically limber, and ready to lead with cognitive clarity and emotional restraint.
*   **Three Job Layers:**
    *   **Functional Job:** Access a guided, 10-15 minute physical stretching and breathing sequence that she can complete in her bedroom before opening email.
    *   **Emotional Job:** Reassure herself that she is taking care of her body and mind, shifting from high-tension anxiety to structured calm.
    *   **Social Job:** Signal to her peers, co-founders, and team that she is a calm, composed, and high-performance leader who does not crack under pressure.
*   **Competing Alternatives:**
    *   *Status Quo:* Doom-scrolling LinkedIn/Slack in bed; drinking a double espresso immediately; taking a 45-minute gym class (takes too long).
    *   *Direct Competitors:* Calm/Headspace (too passive, no physical Asana); Peloton Yoga (too fitness-focused, feels like a workout, lacks mindfulness).
*   **Success Criteria:**
    *   Complete a routine in under 12 minutes without having to configure settings.
    *   Immediate relief of physical neck/back tension.
    *   Feeling a quiet, steady focus before starting work.

### 4. "Day in the Life" Scenario
Sarah's alarm rings at 5:45 AM. Her chest feels tight; she has a board meeting at 9:00 AM. In the past, she would immediately check her phone's notifications, instantly triggering work stress. Today, she opens **Sadhana** instead. 

Using the "Quick Start" widget on her iOS Home Screen, she launches the **15-Min Morning Sadhana** (combining light neck/shoulder Asana, Box Breathing, and a brief grounding Dhyana). She places her iPhone on her nightstand, following the clean, high-contrast video transitions. The screen's *Earth Premium* palette (cream background, subtle olive accents) is gentle on her eyes in the dim morning light. 

By 6:00 AM, she completes the session. An ad triggers, but she has hit her milestone, unlocking a premium sleep breathwork session for tonight. She feels her neck muscles relax and her breathing slow down, entering her day in control.

### 5. Device Preferences & Mobile Constraints
*   **iOS Native Expectations:** She expects fluid swipe-to-back gestures, Haptic Touch feedback on buttons, SF Symbols for iconography, and a dark mode that respects her system settings.
*   **Screen Constraints:** High-density Retina screen. Needs absolute layout precision, smooth SVG rendering, and zero layout shifting during video playback.
*   **Battery/CPU:** Expects zero battery drain when streaming audio in the background while preparing breakfast.

### 6. Sadhana Rewards Relevance
Sarah is highly motivated by milestones (the "gamification" of consistency). 
*   *As a Free User:* She completes sessions to hit the 10-ad milestone, unlocking premium advanced breathwork sessions (e.g., *Nadi Shodhana for Focus*) which she uses before high-stakes meetings. The 10-second interstitial countdown doesn't deter her if it occurs at the end of a session, as she uses that time to hydrate.

## Persona 2: Michael — The "Authenticity Seeker"

```
Name: Michael Tremblay
Age: 45
Occupation: High School History Teacher
Location: Toronto, Ontario (Canada)
Tech Comfort: Moderate (Uses standard web browsers, streaming TV apps, and basic messaging)
Device: Google Pixel 8 (Android 14, 6.2-inch screen)
Subscription Tier: Paid Premium ($14.99/mo)
```

### 1. Visual Photo Description (for Image Generation)
*A high-resolution portrait of a 45-year-old French-Canadian male teacher sitting on a woven meditation cushion in a warm, cozy room filled with books and a small bookshelf. He is smiling gently, looking toward the camera. He has salt-and-pepper hair, a trimmed beard, and is wearing a soft, olive-green linen shirt. A copper water vessel and a small plant sit on the wooden floor next to him. Light pours in from a window on the side (Earth Premium aesthetic).*

### 2. Psychographic Profile (Identity-Need Map)
*   **Dominant Need State:** **Self-Actualization** & **Belonging**. Michael is seeking deep personal growth, inner peace, and a connection to a ancient lineage of wisdom that feels larger than modern secular life.
*   **Identity Commitments:** He views himself as an intellectual, a student of history, and a culturally respectful seeker of truth. He strongly rejects the commercialized, Westernized "fitness-studio" yoga culture, which he views as superficial, noisy, and disrespectful to Eastern roots.
*   **Fears & Friction:**
    *   *Rational Fears:* Incorrect pronunciations of Sanskrit terms; watered-down explanations; commercialized branding; data privacy violations.
    *   *Emotional Threats:* Feeling disconnected from his spiritual goals; feeling that wellness is just another consumerist product.
*   **Worldview & Values:** Ancient wisdom holds the answers to modern alienation. True progress takes time, study, and respect for tradition. Wellness is a sacred practice, not a physical exercise or a productivity hack.

### 3. Jobs-to-be-Done (Progress Job Decomposition)
*   **Hiring Trigger:** Leaving a noisy, crowded school environment at 4:30 PM, feeling intellectually drained and physically exhausted, wishing he could attend a quiet, authentic ashram but having only 30 minutes before preparing dinner.
*   **Progress State:**
    *   *Before:* Overwhelmed by external noise, mentally fragmented, and craving cultural/spiritual depth.
    *   *After:* Intellectually enriched, spiritually connected, and mentally serene, feeling anchored in a lineage of practice.
*   **Three Job Layers:**
    *   **Functional Job:** Stream a guided 25-minute Pranayama and meditation class that explicitly explains the Sanskrit terms, energetic channels (*Nadis*), and scriptural context.
    *   **Emotional Job:** Experience a sense of sacred space, learning the philosophy behind the posture rather than just moving his limbs.
    *   **Social Job:** Align himself with a community of serious practitioners who value authenticity, sharing philosophical quotes with his like-minded peers.
*   **Competing Alternatives:**
    *   *Status Quo:* Reading books on yoga philosophy (lacks guided physical practice); attending local studios (too expensive, dominated by loud music and fitness goals).
    *   *Direct Competitors:* Insight Timer (contains vast options but lacks curated paths, low-quality audio, variable teacher authenticity).
*   **Success Criteria:**
    *   Hearing correct, authentic Sanskrit chanting and translations.
    *   Learning a new philosophical concept during a session.
    *   Experiencing a calm, uncluttered visual interface that respects sacred space (no pop-ups or neon colors).

### 4. "Day in the Life" Scenario
After grading history papers at 5:00 PM, Michael sits on his meditation cushion. He opens **Sadhana** on his Google Pixel 8. 

He selects the **Pranayama Foundation Course**. He appreciates that the app uses a clean, terracotta-and-cream design system instead of flashy, bright blue elements. As he plays the audio, the teacher explains the mechanics of *Ujjayi Pranayama* (ocean breath), detailing its physiological impact on the vagus nerve and its traditional symbolic meaning in the *Hatha Yoga Pradipika*. 

A visual tooltip pops up, showing the Sanskrit spelling (in Devanagari script) and translation. He feels his mind clear and his breathing synchronize. Because he is a Premium user, he completes the session without ads, immediately receiving 30 Karma Coins. He opens the rewards wallet and routes his coins to be donated to a non-profit preserving traditional Indian scriptures, reinforcing his identity as a respectful seeker.

### 5. Device Preferences & Mobile Constraints
*   **Android Native Expectations:** He relies on the system back button/gesture for navigation, expects the app to respect Material You system fonts, and needs edge-to-edge layout styling that doesn't conflict with Android's status or navigation bars.
*   **Screen Constraints:** Pixel 8 OLED display. Highly responsive to dark mode rendering. Needs sharp text scaling because he wears reading glasses.
*   **Offline Requirement:** Often practices in rural parks with weak mobile signals. He demands reliable offline downloading of his favorite audio sessions.

### 6. Sadhana Rewards Relevance
Michael is motivated by altruism and community connection.
*   *As a Premium User:* He views rewarded ads occasionally in the Rewards Tab to earn extra **Karma Coins**, which he accumulates and redeems exclusively for the "Charity Donations" program. This gives him a tangible sense of contributing back to the cultural heritage of the practices he loves, increasing his long-term loyalty to the app.

## Persona 3: Elena — The "Restorative Senior"

```
Name: Elena Weber
Age: 68
Occupation: Retired Librarian
Location: Munich, Bavaria (Germany)
Tech Comfort: Low-Moderate (Uses WhatsApp for family, reads news online, but struggles with complex settings)
Device: Samsung Galaxy A54 (Android 13, 6.4-inch screen)
Subscription Tier: Free (relies on ads to unlock premium classes due to fixed retirement income)
```

### 1. Visual Photo Description (for Image Generation)
*A warm, close-up portrait of a 68-year-old German female with soft, silver-gray hair styled in a bob. She is sitting in a comfortable armchair next to a sunlit window, holding a Samsung phone with both hands, looking at it with a gentle, focused smile. She is wearing a soft cream-colored knitted cardigan. The background shows a cozy room with bookshelves and a small table with a cup of tea (Earth Premium warmth and contrast).*

### 2. Psychographic Profile (Identity-Need Map)
*   **Dominant Need State:** **Security** & **Autonomy**. Elena wants to preserve her physical mobility, manage joint stiffness safely at home, and find emotional peace without fear of falling or injuring herself.
*   **Identity Commitments:** She views herself as an independent, active senior who manages her health proactively. She refuses to be treated as frail, helpless, or out-of-touch. She values quiet, gentle instructions over loud, energetic physical guidance.
*   **Fears & Friction:**
    *   *Rational Fears:* Small text sizes; low contrast; fast-paced instructions; complex nested menus; accidental subscription charges; loss of internet connection.
    *   *Emotional Threats:* Losing her physical independence; isolation; feeling overwhelmed by modern technology.
*   **Worldview & Values:** Gentle consistency is the key to longevity. Health should be nurtured slowly. Technology should be a simple, reliable tool that adapts to human limitations, not a source of confusion.

### 3. Jobs-to-be-Done (Progress Job Decomposition)
*   **Hiring Trigger:** Waking up on a cold morning with stiff knees and lower back discomfort, feeling anxious about her balance during her walk to the local market, and wanting a gentle guide to warm up her joints safely.
*   **Progress State:**
    *   *Before:* Physically stiff, anxious about balance, and feeling cognitive friction due to tech confusion.
    *   *After:* Physically warm, loose, confident in her body's stability, and mentally peaceful.
*   **Three Job Layers:**
    *   **Functional Job:** Follow a slow, audio-centric, chair-based stretching sequence with clear, slow speech and high-contrast visuals.
    *   **Emotional Job:** Gain reassurance that she is safely preserving her physical health, reducing her fear of falling.
    *   **Social Job:** Maintain her autonomy so she does not have to rely on her children or medical assistance for basic physical movement.
*   **Competing Alternatives:**
    *   *Status Quo:* Doing unguided stretches (risk of injury); watching TV senior fitness programs (schedule is not customizable, pacing is often too fast).
    *   *Direct Competitors:* Down Dog (too complex, customization menus are overwhelming for her); YouTube videos (constant ad interruptions during the class, poor audio consistency).
*   **Success Criteria:**
    *   Able to launch a class with exactly two taps from the home screen.
    *   Text is large and easy to read without her reading glasses.
    *   Instructions are slow enough for her to follow without having to constantly look at the screen.

### 4. "Day in the Life" Scenario
Elena wakes up at 7:30 AM. Her lower back feels tight. She makes a pot of herbal tea and sits in her living room. She takes out her Samsung Galaxy A54 and opens **Sadhana**. 

Because she set up the app with the help of her daughter, the typography is automatically set to "Large" format. She taps the prominent green button labeled **"Today's Gentle Asana"** on the home screen. The session begins with slow, soothing audio guidance. The teacher instructs her to sit comfortably in a chair, detailing exactly how to rotate her ankles and shoulders. She doesn't need to look at the screen, allowing her to keep her eyes closed and focus on the physical sensations. 

When the 20-minute class ends, she completes the session. A full-screen ad appears. Knowing that watching this ad contributes to unlocking next week's premium *Joint Mobility Course*, she waits calmly for the 10-second timer to count down. When the large, high-contrast "Close" button appears, she taps it easily, feeling accomplished and loose.

### 5. Device Preferences & Mobile Constraints
*   **Android Accessibility Expectations:** She relies heavily on Android’s system-level text scaling (Dynamic Type). If the app has hardcoded font sizes, it will be unusable for her. Touch targets must be large (minimum 48dp) and far apart to avoid accidental taps.
*   **Screen Constraints:** High-contrast screens are critical. She cannot read thin, light-gray text on a white background. Colors must be bold and clear.
*   **Audio Quality:** Speaker output must be clear, crisp, and high-volume. She cannot follow classes with low-quality audio or heavy background music that drowns out the teacher's voice.

### 6. Sadhana Rewards Relevance
Elena has a limited retirement budget.
*   *As a Free User:* She is highly motivated by the **Milestone Passes**. She watches the post-session ads completely and aims to complete 30 sessions in a month to unlock the **Premium Guided Course Bundle** (specifically the *Restorative Hips and Knees* course) without having to pay cash. This gives her access to high-quality care that she could not otherwise afford, creating immense brand loyalty.

## Psychological Verification Checklist (Zero AI-Slop)

To ensure these personas drive the implementation of future phases, we enforce the following rules on all subsequent code and UI generation:

1.  **Typography & Scaling:** The styling system must support dynamic text scaling (using `rem` or Tailwind `text-` classes properly bound to system font scaling) to support Elena’s accessibility needs.
2.  **Navigation Simplicity:** The Tab Navigation must be flat and shallow. No features critical to Elena or Sarah should be hidden under three levels of nested menus.
3.  **Ad Countdowns:** The 10-second full-screen interstitial countdown must be visually obvious with a clear progress bar to reduce user irritation, aligning with Sarah’s desire for time efficiency and Elena’s need for legibility.
4.  **Sanskrit Typography:** Sanskrit translations and Devanagari text components must be rendered in clear, readable typefaces with toggle-able visibility to avoid cluttering Sarah's screen while satisfying Michael's intellectual needs.
